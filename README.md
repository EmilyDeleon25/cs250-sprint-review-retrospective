How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?

I interpret user needs by breaking down what the user is actually trying to do, not just what they’re saying. 
User stories help a lot with this because they keep everything focused on the user’s goal.
Writing them as “As a user, I want…, so that…” makes it easier to understand the purpose behind
a feature instead of just coding something random. It also helps make sure the final product actually solves a problem and isn’t just technically correct.


How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I usually approach development by breaking things into smaller pieces first, then building and testing step by step. Agile fits really well with that because 
it focuses on working in small iterations instead of trying to do everything at once. Going forward, I’d want to keep using things like sprint planning,
breaking work into user stories, and regular check-ins like stand-ups. I also think getting feedback early is important so you’re not going too far in the wrong direction before realizing it.


What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

To me, being a good team member means communicating clearly and actually working with the team instead of doing everything on your own. 
It’s about sharing progress, asking questions when something isn’t clear, and helping others when needed. It also means being open to feedback and not taking things personally,
since the goal is to improve the project. Overall, just being reliable and making sure the team moves forward together.
